As the coronavirus swung it's bat at Indonesia, we all went crazy within a few days. School's were shutting down unprepared, hospitals were flooding with patients and thousands were loosing their jobs as the days went by. Thankfully, my family and I were safe among other students who went to high class international schools, so we were able to continue school with little to no dificulty online. Unfortunarely, children and families from local communites could not say the same. Recognizing my privilege, I knew it was my duty to use it in aiding those who didn't have access to what I did. Discovering my love of baking during these difficult times, I decided to begin a bakery to raise funds for the local communities affected by the repercussions of the corona virus. Before this project, I had connected with a local NGO called Komunitas Taufan. They focus on working with families who have sick children with long term illnesses such as cancer, who cannot afford to pay for treatment. When COVID-19 hit, a lot of their major donors and volunteers backed out, so they were struggling.
After 4 weeks of rigourous baking, I raised 10 million IDR of profit to donate, in the form of milk powders, medical bills, food etc. On top of that, I was able to get sponsors from various brands including L'oreal, Dettol and craft Heinz. Although this bakery is not a 'non profit' anymore, I am still working to donate 10% of all my profits to similar communities in need, in and around Jakarta.
